Product 
 
These design instructio ns apply to Kerto-RipaВ® elements, which are 
stressed skin panels made of KertoВ® LVL material using one component 
polyurethane adhesive as presented in the ETA-07/0029. The adhesive is 
durable against weather. The pressure  needed in the gluing process is 
obtained by screws or nails. The screws  or nails do not act together with 
the glue bond, thus, they do not carry the shear loads between the web and 
the flange. The flanges are glued to the webs as presen ted in Figure 1. 
 
Figure 1.  An example cross-section of a Kerto-Ripa stressed skin panel. 
There are four types of Kert o-Ripa elements available: 
- A Kerto-Ripa ribbed slab which consists of only one upper (i.e. 
top) slab, or one lower (i.e. botto m) slab. The latter case means 
that the ribbed slab is used upside down.  
- A Kerto-Ripa box slab which has both top and bottom slabs.

A. Design methods of Kerto-Ripa elements 
 
1 Application area of these instructions 
This document presents the design methods of Kerto-Ripa elements 
defined in the ETA-07/0029. The design methods are based on the 
standard EN1995-1-1:2004+AC:2006+ A1:2008+A2:2014 (EC 5) and 
general principles of structural mechanics. 
 
2 Calculation principles 
According to the ETA-07/0029, the design calculations of Kerto-Ripa 
elements should be made by using th e sanded thicknesses of the slabs.  
The slabs of Kerto-Ripa elements ar e connected to th e ribs by gluing. 
Thus, the slip between the slab and the rib is assumed to be zero. In 
addition, the theory of the thin-flanged beams pr esented in the EC 5 
standard is used. 
The structural checking of the Kerto-Ripa element comprises of separate 
design checkings of each rib and its  effective cross-section in the 
longitudinal direction of the elemen t. The design criteria of these 
calculations are presented in the Chapters 3 to 10 of the Section A of this 
document.  
The bending stiffness of each effective cross-section of the element is 
calculated according to the effective flange width b ef,. The parameter b ef 
is calculated in each span accordi ng to the effective span length L ef. With 
continuous elements, L ef is assumed to be the same as L i, as presented in 
Figure A1.

Kerto-Ripa element.

well as for deflection. These checking s are not included in  this document. 
Optionally plywood plate can be used with Kerto-Ripa element. Plywood 
plate can act as lateral torsional buck ling support for ribs  but it shall not 
be included in the composite cross- section. Clearance between plywood 
plated needs to be big enough to allow moisture and compression 
deformation. 
Nevertheless, the following assumptions shall be used:  1) The deflection of a structurally glue d single-span plate can be reduced 
by 23 %, when the plate is simply supported, under uniformly distributed loading and the ribs ar e connected to each other at the 
opposite edge with spacing c/c вүӨ 600 mm. For multi-span plate 
reduction is not allowed. 
2) When calculating the Kerto-Q plate in the transverse direction, the 
nominal thickness without sanding is used (for example 27 mm 
instead of 25 mm). 
3) Shear deflection shall be taken into  account in the deflection checking.

10.3 Transverse stiffness 
The transverse stiffness EI B (i.e. the stiffness perpendicular to the span 
direction) of the element is primarily calculated based on the EI B of the 
slabs. Additional stiffness from blocking s, floating floor, etc. is evaluated 
case by case. When blockings are used as transverse bracings, these are made of the same Kerto-S size as the ribs of the element. Further 
information on blockings is presented be low in this chapter and in Chapter 
11.1. 
In Kerto-Ripa ribbed slabs, blockings are connected to the top slab of the 
element and to a tension chord (made of 22Г—100 sawn timber minimum) 
at the bottom of the element by nails 2.8Г—75 c/c 200 (minimum). The tension chord is also nailed to the ri bs of the element with the same nails 
(2 nails 2.8Г—75 per rib).  
In Kerto-Ripa open box slabs, bloc kings are connected to the top and 
bottom slabs of the element by nails 2.8Г—75 c/c 200 (minimum). The 
tension chord (made of 22Г—100 sawn timber minimum) is then connected 
to the bottom of the bottom slab at the ribs with the same nails (2 nails 2.8Г—75 per rib).  
Due to the tension chords, adjacent Kerto-Ripa ribbed and open box slabs 
are connected to each other both at  the top and at the bottom of the 
element. The joints of the tension chord must, however, not be located at 
the joints of the adjacent Kerto-Ripa slabs. See Chapter 11.3 for more 
information on connections between adjacent elements.

11 Structural details of Kerto-Ripa elements 
11.1 Details at supports  
Kerto-Ripa elements shall have end beams or blocking lines at both ends 
of an element, and at intermediate supports. End beams are continuous beams connected to the ends of the ribs, and blocking lines consist of short 
beams (blockings) assembled between the ribs. Both structures have a 
height between 0.6 - 1.0 times the he ight of the ribs, and run through the 
element in the transverse direction. 
End beams are made of Kerto-S, Ke rto-Q, Kerto-Kate or plywood and 
blocking lines are made of Kerto-S or Kerto-Q beams which are nailed, 
screwed, glued, or nailed/screwed and glued to the ribs and slab(s) of the 
element. The structural detail of  the element and its design method 
determine the Kerto product to be used in these cases.  
See Chapter 10.3 of this section A for more information about blocking 
lines as transverse bracing of the Kerto-Ripa elements.

D. Support reinforced Kerto-Ripa elements 
1 Application area of this annex 
This annex concerns the support reinforcements of Ripa elements 
according to ETA-07/0029. The resistance of the following is considered 
according to the Eurocode 5 in service classes 1 and 2 ultimate limit state 
design.

perpendicular to the grain by structur ally gluing reinforcement pieces to 
the ribs. Not the reinforcement piece nor  the rib need to be sanded for the 
following design instructions. These re inforcements shall be made from 
Kerto-S and they may be single or double sided. If double sided 
reinforcement is used, the pieces sha ll be same size and thicknesses. At 
intermediate support the reinforcem ent pieces shall be positioned 
centrally to the centre lin e of the support. There sh all always be at least
